# Robot Vacuum + Vacuum-Mop Combo Maintenance Guide (200+ Entries)

Categories: General Basics / Vacuuming-Specific / Mopping-Specific / Consumables / Environment Adaptation / Long-Term Storage / Fault Prevention — Covering All Model Types

## General Basic Maintenance (50 Entries)

1. After daily use, wipe the robot body with a dry soft cloth to remove dust and water stains, preventing stains from hardening.
2. After each cleaning session, promptly clear hair and threads from bumper strip gaps to prevent clip jamming that affects rebound.
3. Daily check the bottom drive wheels and caster wheel, use tweezers to remove tangled hair and debris, ensuring smooth wheel rotation.
4. During daily use, avoid the robot colliding with sharp hard objects (e.g., table corners, metal racks) to prevent body and sensor damage.
5. Daily clean the charging contacts by wiping the metal contacts on both the robot and charging dock with a dry cloth to prevent oxidation causing poor contact.
6. While the robot is working, avoid touching the rotating main brush or side brushes by hand to prevent injury and component damage.
7. Daily check the bottom anti-drop and edge-following sensors, gently wipe surfaces with a dry cloth, ensuring no dust or water stains.
8. When cleaning the robot, always disconnect power. Never rinse the body directly with water (waterproof models may only be wiped, not submerged).
9. Daily clear debris from the dustbin latch area, ensuring proper installation to prevent dust leakage and alarms.
10. After each use, return the robot to the charging dock to maintain charge, avoiding deep-discharge storage.
11. Weekly check body screws; tighten any loose ones promptly to prevent components from falling off.
12. Weekly clean heat dissipation vents with a soft brush to prevent dust blockage causing motor thermal protection.
13. Weekly press the bumper strip several times to check rebound; if stuck, disassemble and clear internal debris promptly.
14. Weekly wipe the APP connection indicator light and buttons to prevent dust coverage affecting operation and display.
15. Monthly apply 1-2 drops of specialized lubricant to caster and drive wheel axles to prevent rust and noise (do not over-apply to avoid attracting dust).
16. Monthly inspect the battery compartment, confirm no battery swelling or leakage, and ensure cable connections are secure.
17. Monthly wipe obstacle avoidance camera/sensors with a dry cloth to prevent dust and fingerprints from affecting recognition accuracy.
18. Monthly perform a thorough dust removal on the entire robot body, including the bottom, back, charging port, and other hard-to-reach areas.
19. When the robot makes unusual noises, immediately stop and inspect. Never force continued use, which could worsen the fault.
20. Before replacing any components, always disconnect power and confirm the part model matches the robot model to avoid mixing.
21. Avoid operating the robot in high-temperature (>40°C) or low-temperature (<0°C) environments for extended periods to prevent battery and motor damage.
22. Avoid the robot contacting corrosive liquids (e.g., alcohol, disinfectant, dish soap) to prevent body and component aging.
23. After operation, if the body has grease stains, gently wipe with diluted neutral detergent, then dry with a cloth.
24. Daily check that the charging dock is placed on a level surface with no surrounding obstructions to ensure smooth return-to-charge.
25. Weekly clean the charging dock infrared sensing area with a dry cloth to prevent dust from affecting return-to-charge signal reception.
26. Avoid squeezing or tangling the charging dock power cable; weekly check the plug and cable for damage to prevent short circuits.
27. The robot should not work or be stored in direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent shell discoloration and sensor light anomalies.
28. Monthly check main brush and side brush clips, confirm no breakage or looseness, ensuring secure component installation.
29. When moving the robot, handle gently. Never drag or drop it to prevent internal component displacement.
30. Weekly check the dustbin air outlet, ensuring no blockage, to maintain normal suction.
31. During operation, if the robot encounters large debris (e.g., stones, bottle caps), manually remove them promptly to prevent jamming.
32. Monthly clean debris from the suction inlet with a soft brush to prevent blockage affecting vacuum performance.
33. Daily check for visible scratches or deformation; if collision caused deformation, contact after-sales for correction promptly.
34. Avoid applying overly adhesive stickers to the robot body; removal may leave adhesive residue that corrodes the shell.
35. Weekly wipe the dust collection port (auto-empty models), ensuring a tight seal to prevent dust leakage.
36. During use, avoid floors with standing water to prevent water from entering the body and causing short circuits.
37. Monthly check the mopping module interface (vacuum-mop combos), ensuring no dust or debris, and smooth connection.
38. After daily use, set the robot's cleaning mode to "standby" to reduce power consumption.
39. Weekly check the charging dock power adapter for overheating or unusual sounds, avoiding prolonged overload operation.
40. If the robot accidentally contacts pet waste, immediately clean with wet wipes, then wipe with neutral detergent to prevent bacterial growth.
41. Daily check side brush rotation; if stuck, immediately stop and clean to prevent motor burnout.
42. Avoid operating the robot near high drop-off areas (e.g., stairways); even with anti-drop features, take precautions.
43. Monthly perform a "no-load test" by running the robot for 1 minute without load to check for unusual sounds or jamming.
44. Weekly wipe silicone components (e.g., seal rings, bumper strips) with a dry cloth to prevent aging and cracking.
45. Daily clear debris from the roller chamber entrance to prevent it from entering and tangling the main brush.
46. Avoid using hard brushes or steel wool to clean robot components to prevent surface scratches and sensor damage.
47. Weekly check the robot's APP connection status, ensuring normal network connectivity to receive consumable and fault notifications.
48. When charging the robot, ensure a well-ventilated environment free from flammable or explosive materials.
49. Monthly check the anti-tangle comb teeth (if equipped), ensuring no deformation or breakage, functioning normally.
50. Follow the "gentle handling, timely cleaning" principle in daily use to reduce component wear and extend overall lifespan.

## Vacuuming-Specific Maintenance (30 Entries)

1. After each cleaning session, immediately clear hair and threads from the main brush. Use scissors to cut tangled material; never pull forcefully.
2. Daily clean dust and debris from the side brushes to ensure they can properly sweep corner dust into the suction inlet during rotation.
3. Weekly disassemble the main roller brush chamber and clean internal dust and hair with a soft brush, leaving no residue in any corners.
4. If the main brush "slips" during use, check whether the roller brush rubber strip is worn; clean or replace promptly.
5. Weekly clean the robot's air duct by sweeping from the dustbin air outlet toward the suction inlet with a soft brush to ensure airflow is unobstructed.
6. For auto-empty models, daily inspect the dust collection channel for hair or debris blockage to ensure smooth dust collection.
7. After each dustbin cleaning, wipe the inside of the dustbin with a dry cloth to prevent dust clumping. Do not soak in water (except for waterproof dustbins).
8. The dustbin filter must be installed together with the dustbin; it must not be missing, to prevent dust from entering the motor and causing damage.
9. If suction decreases during vacuuming, first check whether the dustbin, filter, or air duct is clogged; clean each one and retry.
10. Weekly check the suction inlet roller brush (for models without a main brush), ensuring no tangling or wear, and smooth rotation.
11. For auto-empty models, daily check that the dust collection bag is properly installed and sealed tightly to prevent dust leakage.
12. After vacuuming long-pile carpets, immediately clean carpet fibers from the main brush to prevent tangling and hardening.
13. Monthly check the main brush motor's operating condition; there should be no abnormal heating or noise during operation. If detected, stop immediately.
14. When cleaning the main brush, take care to protect the bearings at both ends of the roller brush; avoid water or dust ingress that causes jamming.
15. When side brush bristles are worn to less than 1 cm in length, replace immediately to avoid compromising corner cleaning performance.
16. Weekly clean the robot's dust sensors with a soft brush to prevent misjudgment of ambient dust levels causing suction anomalies.
17. For auto-empty models, weekly clean the auto-empty station's air outlet to ensure no blockage and maintain proper dust collection suction.
18. After vacuuming on rough surfaces such as cement or tile, promptly clean sand and grit from the robot's underside to prevent wheel assembly wear.
19. After each cleaning session, empty the dustbin completely to avoid bacteria growth and odors from prolonged storage.
20. Monthly check the robot's suction adjustment module to ensure all suction levels switch normally without jamming.
21. When clearing hair, if the main brush has an anti-tangle design, use the comb teeth to quickly strip hair off, improving cleaning efficiency.
22. If the robot supports "carpet boost," check after use that the boost module has reset properly, with no jamming or unusual sounds.
23. Weekly check side brush clips to ensure side brushes are securely installed and do not wobble or shift during rotation.
24. If the dustbin has an odor, place an activated carbon packet inside to absorb it, or gently wipe with a neutral deodorizer. Allow to dry before use.
25. After using the robot in a pet environment, immediately clean the main brush and dustbin to prevent pet hair from clumping.
26. Monthly disassemble the suction inlet assembly and clean internal fine debris to ensure the suction inlet remains unobstructed.
27. For auto-empty models, turn off power before replacing the dust collection bag to prevent dust leakage during the collection process.
28. If the main roller brush has a detachable design, remove and clean it once a week (wipe with a dry cloth for non-waterproof models). Allow to dry before reinstalling.
29. After vacuuming large debris, immediately inspect the suction inlet and roller brush chamber for residue to prevent jamming.
30. Monthly check the robot's motor cooling fan for dust blockage to ensure proper motor heat dissipation.

## Vacuum-Mop Combo Mopping-Specific Maintenance (40 Entries)

1. After each mopping session, immediately remove the mop cloth, rinse thoroughly with clean water, wring out, and hang in a ventilated area to dry, preventing mold.
2. Daily clean the mop bracket, using a soft brush to remove tangled hair and threads, keeping the bracket clean and debris-free.
3. Before each use of the water tank, check for impurities or scale inside; fill with purified water to avoid scale buildup from direct tap water.
4. After mopping, drain remaining water from the tank, wipe the interior and water outlet with a dry cloth to prevent scale blockage.
5. Weekly soak the water tank in citric acid solution (1:50 dilution) for 10 minutes to remove scale, then rinse with clean water and air dry.
6. For auto-wash mop models, after each use, empty the dirty water tank. Rinse the dirty water tank and filter with clean water, leaving no stain residue.
7. For auto-wash mop models, daily clean the cleaning station's cleaning brush. Remove hair and debris to ensure effective mop washing.
8. Monthly apply 1 drop of specialized lubricant to the mop bracket pivot to prevent rust and jamming, ensuring smooth mop rotation.
9. Before installing the mop cloth each time, check that it is clean and not hardened, ensuring no secondary contamination during mopping.
10. Weekly wipe the water tank seal ring with a dry cloth; check for aging, cracking, or deformation to ensure a watertight seal.
11. For auto-wash mop models, weekly clean the cleaning station's water level sensor. Gently wipe with a dry cloth to prevent stains from causing false readings.
12. If water output is abnormal during mopping, immediately clean the water tank outlet and tubing. Use a toothpick to gently unclog, avoiding blockage.
13. For electric mop models, after each use check the mop motor for abnormal heating or noise; promptly clear debris from around the motor.
14. If the mop cloth has an odor, soak in warm water with neutral detergent for 15 minutes, scrub clean, scald once with boiling water, then air dry before use.
15. Before installing the water tank each time, check the water tank interface on the robot body for dust or debris, ensuring smooth and leak-free connection.
16. For auto-lift mop models, daily check the lift module to ensure no jamming or misalignment, and that lifting and lowering functions work normally.
17. Disposable mop pads should be discarded immediately after use. Do not reuse them, as they may shed fibers and clog the mopping module.
18. Weekly wipe the underside of the mopping module to remove water stains and dirt, preventing bacterial growth and odors.
19. For auto-wash mop models, monthly clean the cleaning station's water pump tubing with descaling agent to prevent scale blockage causing no water output.
20. When adding cleaning solution to the water tank, strictly dilute according to the specified ratio. Never add concentrated cleaning solution directly to prevent corrosion of the tank and tubing.
21. After mopping, wipe the mopping module interface on the robot's underside with a dry cloth, ensuring no water stains or dust residue.
22. Weekly check the mop cloth's adhesive/clip design to ensure no loosening or detachment, and that the mop is securely installed.
23. For auto-wash mop models, after each use clean the cleaning station's drain outlet to ensure no debris blockage and smooth drainage.
24. If the mop cloth starts shedding fibers during use, replace it immediately to prevent lint from remaining on the floor and affecting cleaning results.
25. Monthly check the water tank's outlet valve to ensure normal open/close operation, no jamming or leaking, and smooth water flow.
26. Keep the mopping module's plastic components away from high temperatures and corrosive liquids to prevent aging and deformation.
27. For auto-wash mop models, weekly check the dirty water tank filter for blockage or damage, ensuring proper filtration and preventing dirty water backflow.
28. Before each mopping session, gently pat the mop cloth to remove loose dust, then install and use, preventing loose dust from contaminating the floor.
29. When the water tank will not be used for an extended period, wipe the seal ring with a dry cloth and apply a thin layer of silicone grease to prevent aging.
30. For electric mop models, monthly check the mop rotation speed; ensure no slowdown or jamming. If detected, clean or repair promptly.
31. If mopping leaves scratches on the floor, immediately check whether sand grains or hard objects are trapped in the mop cloth; clean before continuing use.
32. For auto-wash mop models, daily check the cleaning station's clean water inlet for dust or debris blockage, ensuring smooth filling.
33. Weekly clean stains and scale from the mop bracket with a soft brush to prevent hardened buildup that becomes difficult to remove.
34. After mopping, remove the mop bracket and place it in a ventilated area to dry, avoiding bacterial growth from contact with the robot body.
35. Monthly check the mopping module's wiring and cables, ensuring no damage or looseness, preventing short circuits and poor connections.
36. If debris accidentally enters the water tank, immediately drain the water, use tweezers to remove the debris, then rinse thoroughly with clean water.
37. For auto-wash mop models, after use wipe the cleaning station's exterior with a dry cloth to remove water stains and dirt, keeping it clean.
38. After 3-5 uses, sterilize the mop cloth once with high-temperature steam to kill bacteria and dust mites, especially suitable for households with pets or babies.
39. Monthly check the motor of auto-lift mop models for abnormal heating during operation. If detected, contact after-sales service for inspection promptly.
40. All mopping module components must be fully dried after disassembly and cleaning before reinstallation, preventing water ingress that damages motors and circuits.

## Consumable Maintenance & Replacement (30 Entries)

1. HEPA filter: Weekly brush surface dust with a soft brush. Every 1-2 months, rinse with clean water (do not rub). Air dry in a cool place. Replace every 3-6 months.
2. Main brush: Replace every 3-6 months for regular households. For pet households or long-pile carpet environments, replace every 1-3 months. Replace immediately if worn, deformed, or cracked.
3. Side brushes: Replace when bristles are worn to less than 1 cm, deformed, or broken. Generally replace every 2-3 months; every 1 month in dusty environments.
4. Dustbin filter (primary): Daily clean surface dust. Monthly rinse with clean water; air dry before use. Replace every 6 months.
5. Mop cloth (washable): Replace every 3-6 months under normal use. For pet or heavy-grease environments, replace every 1-2 months. Replace immediately if hardened, shedding fibers, or damaged.
6. Water tank seal ring: Replace every 6-12 months. If aging, cracking, or leaking occurs, replace immediately without waiting.
7. Dust collection bag (auto-empty models): Replace every 1-2 months for regular households. For dusty or pet households, replace every 0.5-1 month. Replace promptly when 80% full.
8. Cleaning brush (auto-wash mop models): Weekly clear hair. Monthly rinse with clean water. Replace every 3-6 months. Replace immediately if worn or deformed.
9. Caster wheel/drive wheels: Replace when wheel surfaces are severely worn, rotation is jammed, or noise occurs. Generally replace every 1-2 years; every 6-12 months on rough surfaces.
10. Roller brush rubber strip (main brush accessory): Replace when worn, cracked, or peeling. Generally replace on the same schedule as the main brush.
11. Dirty water tank filter (vacuum-mop combos): Weekly rinse with clean water; air dry before use. Replace every 6 months. Replace immediately if damaged.
12. Battery: Replace every 2-3 years under normal use. If battery life drops below 50% of original capacity, won't charge, or shows swelling, replace with an original battery immediately.
13. Disposable mop pads: Single use only. Replace after each use; do not reuse.
14. Anti-tangle comb teeth: Replace when deformed or broken. Generally inspect every 6-12 months; every 3 months for pet households.
15. Charging contact copper plates: If oxidation or rust appears, gently clean with rust remover. If not repairable, replace the contact components.
16. Mopping module cleaning brush (vacuum-mop combos): Replace on the same schedule as the mop cloth. Replace immediately if worn or shedding fibers.
17. Air duct filter: Clean every 3-6 months. Replace every 1-2 years. Replace sooner if severely clogged.
18. Side brush clips: Replace with the same original clips immediately when broken, loose, or unable to secure the side brush.
19. Mop bracket: Replace with original bracket when clips are broken, deformed, or pivot jamming cannot be repaired.
20. Auto-empty station filter (auto-empty models): Monthly clean. Replace every 6 months. Replace sooner if damaged or severely clogged.
21. Suction inlet roller brush (models without main brush): Same replacement cycle as the main brush — every 3-6 months; every 1-3 months for pet households.
22. Water tank outlet valve: Replace with original outlet valve component when leaking or jammed and not repairable.
23. Bumper strip: Replace with original bumper strip when cracked, detached, or rebound is abnormal. Do not substitute with generic adhesive strips.
24. Cleaning solution: Use within 3 months after opening. Store sealed in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
25. Lubricant: Use robot-specific lubricant only. Do not substitute with motor oil, grease, or other alternatives to prevent dust adhesion and component damage.
26. Mop Velcro tabs: When adhesion decreases and the mop cloth cannot be secured, replace Velcro components or replace the entire mop bracket.
27. Robot foot pads: Replace when severe wear causes body wobbling, ensuring stable operation.
28. Power cable (charging dock): Replace with original power cable immediately when damaged, aged, or overheating. Do not use non-original cables.
29. Mop wash water pump filter (vacuum-mop combos): Weekly clean. Replace every 6 months. Replace sooner if blockage causes no water output.
30. Always prioritize **original replacement parts** for all consumables. Non-original parts with mismatched dimensions or specifications can easily cause equipment malfunction and reduced cleaning performance.

## Environment Adaptation Maintenance (20 Entries)

1. Hardwood floor environment: Set mopping to low water output; use dry-mop mode to avoid standing water on floors. Weekly remove dust from the robot's wheel assembly to prevent sand from scratching floors.
2. Ceramic tile/marble environment: Promptly clean water stains from the robot's underside after mopping. Monthly descale the mop bracket to prevent scale buildup.
3. Long-pile carpet environment: Immediately clean carpet fibers from the main brush and side brushes after vacuuming. Disable mopping to prevent the mop cloth from soaking the carpet and causing mold.
4. Short-pile carpet environment: After using the carpet boost function, check that the main brush has reset. Weekly clean dust from carpet edges to prevent the robot from missing spots.
5. Cement/old tile floor environment: Immediately clean sand and grit from the robot's underside and wheel assembly after vacuuming to prevent component wear. Reduce mopping frequency to avoid dust clumping.
6. Pet households: Thoroughly clean the main brush, dustbin, and mop cloth after each cleaning session. Weekly deodorize and disinfect the robot. Reduce consumable replacement intervals by 50%.
7. Households with babies/young children: Use only clean water for mopping; avoid adding cleaning solutions. Sterilize the mop cloth at high temperature after each use. Daily wipe the robot body.
8. Dusty/sandy environments: Daily clean the dustbin and filter. Weekly replace side brushes. Disable mopping to prevent dust and water mixing into clumps.
9. Southern humid/monsoon season environments: Reduce mopping frequency; use dry-mop mode. Daily air-dry the robot body and consumables. Weekly perform dehumidification treatment on the robot.
10. Northern dry/dusty environments: Increase suction level. Daily clean the dustbin. Monthly perform deep cleaning of the air duct and filter. Set mopping to medium water output.
11. Kitchen heavy-grease environments: Immediately clean the mop cloth and water tank after mopping. Wash the mop cloth with neutral detergent. Weekly descale the water tank to prevent grease blockage.
12. Bathroom environment: Vacuum only; do not mop. Prevent the robot from contacting standing water. Wipe the robot's underside after cleaning to ensure no water stains remain.
13. Enclosed balcony environment: Open windows for ventilation promptly after mopping. Clean sand and grit from the robot's wheel assembly to avoid dust and water mixing on the balcony.
14. Multi-story/duplex homes: Create separate maps for each floor. Before switching floors, clean the robot's underside and sensors, and recalibrate vacuuming and mopping parameters.
15. Environments with mirrors/glass furniture: Clean the robot's obstacle avoidance sensors before vacuuming to prevent reflections from causing mapping errors. Weekly wipe sensors.
16. Low-clearance furniture areas: After the robot enters, manually assist with cleaning dead zones. Weekly clean bottom sensors to prevent dust obstruction.
17. Environments with door thresholds/steps: After the robot crosses, check the wheel assembly and mopping module for jamming. Manually assist with cleaning dust at thresholds.
18. Summer high-temperature environment: Avoid operating the robot in direct sunlight. Ensure a well-ventilated charging environment. Weekly check battery temperature to prevent overheating.
19. Winter low-temperature environment: Let the robot sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before operation to prevent reduced battery life. Ensure ambient temperature is ≥5°C when charging.
20. Post-renovation cleaning environment: Manually perform initial cleanup first — clear cement residue, sand, and sharp debris before using the robot to prevent jamming and scratching components.

## Long-Term Storage Maintenance (20 Entries)

1. Before storage, fully charge the robot to 80%-90% battery level. Avoid storing with a depleted battery to prevent battery damage.
2. Before storage, thoroughly clean the entire robot body, dustbin, filter, main brush, side brushes, mop cloth, and all accessories, leaving no dust or stain residue.
3. For vacuum-mop combo robots, drain all water from the water tank and dirty water tank before storage. Wipe dry with a cloth and allow to air dry before storing.
4. Remove all detachable accessories (e.g., mop cloth, mop bracket, dust collection bag, filter). Wash, dry, and store separately to prevent bacterial growth from contact with the robot body.
5. Before storage, thoroughly wipe the battery compartment, charging contacts, and sensors to ensure no dust or water stains.
6. Store the robot in a cool, dry, well-ventilated environment. Avoid high temperatures, low temperatures, humidity, and direct sunlight.
7. Maintain storage temperature between 5°C-35°C with relative humidity ≤60%. Keep away from flammable, explosive, and corrosive materials.
8. During storage, disconnect the robot from the charging dock. Do not leave it plugged in for extended charging to prevent battery overcharge damage.
9. Store accessories individually in sealed bags with desiccant packets to prevent moisture, mold, and aging.
10. Before storing the water tank seal ring, apply a thin layer of silicone grease and place in a sealed bag to prevent aging and cracking.
11. For long-term storage (>1 month), recharge the robot to 80%-90% every 1-2 months to prevent battery deep discharge.
12. During storage, keep the robot body flat. Do not store upside down or on its side to prevent internal component displacement or battery leakage.
13. Avoid placing heavy objects on the robot during storage to prevent deformation of the body shell and accessories.
14. Store the charging dock separately from the robot. Neatly coil the power cable and protect the plug from dust entry.
15. Before resuming use after long-term storage, first check whether the battery is swollen or leaking, and whether body accessories are loose or aged.
16. Before resuming use, thoroughly clean and dry all robot accessories. Replace any expired or aged consumables.
17. Before resuming use, run the robot unloaded for 1-2 minutes to check for unusual sounds or jamming, and test all functions.
18. Before resuming use, connect to the charging dock and charge for 30 minutes to activate the battery and calibrate the charge display.
19. Before resuming use, delete old maps and let the robot remap. Recalibrate vacuuming, mopping, obstacle avoidance, and return-to-charge functions.
20. If the robot is stored in a dark environment such as a garage or storage room, disinfect the robot before storage and include pest and rodent deterrent accessories.

## Fault Prevention Maintenance (20 Entries)

1. Regularly update the robot firmware and APP to ensure normal system functionality and reduce operational anomalies caused by software faults.
2. Avoid extended continuous operation of the robot. For large homes, clean in batches to prevent motor and battery overheating faults.
3. Before each use, check the floor for sharp hard objects, wires, ropes, etc. to prevent component jamming and accessory tangling.
4. Strictly follow the model instructions. Do not exceed load capacity. Avoid operating the robot in environments exceeding its rated cleaning area.
5. Follow the "shallow charge, shallow discharge" principle when charging to avoid battery overcharge and over-discharge, extending battery life and reducing battery faults.
6. Promptly clean clogged filters and dustbins to prevent suction loss that causes the motor to work under overload, leading to motor burnout.
7. If the mop cloth or water tank leaks, immediately stop the robot. Clean up before resuming use to prevent water entering the body and causing circuit short circuits.
8. When the robot exhibits slight unusual sounds or jamming, immediately stop and inspect. Never force continued use, which could escalate the fault.
9. Avoid disassembling the robot's internal components (e.g., motor, motherboard, battery) yourself. Contact official after-sales service for repairs after a fault.
10. Monthly perform a comprehensive function test on the robot, including vacuuming, mopping, obstacle avoidance, return-to-charge, and spot cleaning, to detect potential faults early.
11. Replace consumables on schedule. Avoid allowing aged or worn consumables to cause abnormal robot operation and damage to other components.
12. When connecting the robot to WiFi, use only 2.4 GHz networks. Avoid 5 GHz networks that may cause connection instability and functional anomalies.
13. Regularly clear the robot's cache (via the APP) to prevent excessive cache causing APP lag and command transmission failures.
14. Avoid charging the robot in humid environments to prevent charging contact oxidation and short circuits that cause charging faults.
15. If the robot accidentally gets submerged in water, immediately disconnect power and place in a ventilated area to dry. Do not power on. Contact after-sales for inspection.
16. Weekly check the robot's wiring and cables for damage or looseness to prevent short circuits and poor connections causing faults.
17. For auto-wash mop models, avoid running the cleaning station's water pump dry without water to prevent damage to the pump and heating module (if equipped).
18. For auto-empty models, avoid continued use after the dust collection bag is full to prevent dust from entering the motor, causing suction loss and motor faults.
19. When the robot returns to charge, ensure no obstructions around the charging dock. Avoid repeated return-to-charge attempts that cause motor and battery wear.
20. Establish a robot maintenance log. Record consumable replacement dates and fault handling details. Regularly summarize maintenance experience to proactively prevent recurring faults.
