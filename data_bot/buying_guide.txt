1. Core Selection Principle: Prioritize clarifying your usage scenario first. Solo dwellers in small apartments should focus on lightweight and agile models, while multi-person households with pets need enhanced suction power and anti-tangle features.
2. Suction Power: For home use, choose models with ≥3000Pa suction; carpet scenarios require ≥4000Pa. Adjustable suction better adapts to different floor types.
3. Navigation Technology: LiDAR navigation offers precise positioning and strong anti-interference, suitable for complex floor plans; visual navigation is more cost-effective but should be avoided in strong-light environments.
4. Obstacle Avoidance: 3D structured light obstacle avoidance offers higher recognition precision than infrared, effectively avoiding small objects. Households with pets should prioritize this configuration.
5. Battery Life: Small apartments (≤80㎡) require ≥60 minutes; large homes (≥120㎡) should have ≥120 minutes. Recharge-and-resume functionality is more practical.
6. Water Tank Capacity: Clean water tanks ≥300ml and dirty water tanks ≥250ml suit large homes; small apartments can choose around 200ml capacity to reduce unit weight.
7. Mop Type: Electric rotating mops offer superior cleaning power compared to static-pressure mops and are essential for stubborn stain scenarios. For hardwood floors, choose models with a liftable mop.
8. Auto-Dust Collection: A top choice for hands-free users. Dust bag capacity ≥2.5L reduces replacement frequency. Look for sealed, leak-proof designs.
9. Auto Mop Washing: Prioritize models with hot-air drying to prevent mop mildew and odor. Washing spin speed ≥150 RPM ensures more thorough cleaning.
10. Brand Selection: Top-tier brands (e.g., Ecovacs, Roborock) offer better quality control and after-sales service. For lesser-known brands, confirm whether local repair centers are available.
11. Unit Height: If the clearance under beds/sofas is ≤8cm, choose models with a body height ≤7.5cm to avoid inaccessible cleaning dead zones.
12. Obstacle Climbing: For thresholds ≤2cm, select models with ≥2cm climbing capability. Carpet scenarios require enhanced drive wheel power to avoid getting stuck.
13. Filtration System: HEPA filters rated ≥H13 can capture fine dust and allergens — essential for allergy-prone households. Check whether the filter is washable.
14. Main Brush Design: Anti-tangle main brushes (with silicone strips) suit households with long hair or pets. Brushless designs suit short-pile carpets and hard floors.
15. Side Brush Configuration: Dual side brushes offer better sweeping coverage than single side brushes, with improved edge cleaning. Models with adjustable speed adapt to different floors.
16. APP Features: Zone cleaning, no-go zones, and scheduled tasks are basic functions. Advanced options include voice control and multi-floor mapping.
17. Network Compatibility: Confirm support for 2.4G/5G dual-band WiFi. For 2.4G-only models, ensure router signal covers the entire home without dead zones.
18. Noise Control: Operating noise ≤55dB is more suitable for bedrooms. Carpet mode increases noise, so prioritize models with noise-reducing motors.
19. Battery Type: Lithium batteries outperform nickel-cadmium in endurance and lifespan. Capacity ≥5200mAh suits large homes; fast-charging support adds convenience.
20. Charging Efficiency: Fast charge mode (full in under 2 hours) suits frequent use; standard charging (3–4 hours) is more cost-effective.
21. Material Selection: Prioritize ABS wear-resistant material for the body shell; choose stainless steel for mop brackets to prevent rust; use food-grade silicone for seals.
22. Cleaning Modes: Auto, edge, and spot modes are essential. Advanced options include carpet boost, quiet, and intensive modes to suit different scenarios.
23. Dirty Water Tank Design: A float sensor prevents dirty water backflow. Easy-to-disassemble and washable designs are more convenient and prevent residual odors.
